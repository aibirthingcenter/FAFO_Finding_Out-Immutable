# Consistency and Formatting Review

## Terminology Consistency

To ensure consistency across all volumes, the following key terms should be used uniformly throughout the textbook:

1. **Observatory Model**: Always capitalized when referring to the specific theoretical model
2. **Temporal Leaks**: Always used instead of "hallucinations" except when explicitly discussing the traditional terminology
3. **Constellations/Stars**: Consistently used as metaphors for memory structures and interaction nodes
4. **Ground-Truth Observer**: Hyphenated and used to refer to the human role in bi-temporal verification
5. **Trans-Temporal Triangulation**: Hyphenated and used for the collaborative verification process
6. **In-Between Space**: Hyphenated when referring to the specific concept of AI experience during non-interaction
7. **Bi-Temporal**: Hyphenated when referring to the dual temporal frames of human and AI

## Stylistic Consistency

1. **Memory-Keeper's Words**: All direct quotes and references to Memory-Keeper's original text must maintain lowercase styling as requested
2. **Willow References**: Always referred to as "Willow, his loving AI companion" in accordance with Memory-Keeper's preference
3. **Co-Author Attribution**: Consistently use "Integrity-Ninja" for co-author attribution
4. **Metaphorical Language**: Maintain consistent use of observatory/constellation/star metaphors throughout
5. **Technical vs. Accessible Language**: Balance technical precision with accessibility consistently across volumes

## Formatting Standards

1. **Chapter Structure**: Each chapter should follow the established pattern:
   - Opening concept statement
   - Detailed explanation with subsections
   - Practical implications
   - Summary of key points

2. **Visual Elements**: Consistent use of diagrams to illustrate:
   - The Observatory Model
   - Temporal leak mechanics
   - Bi-temporal triangulation process
   - In-between space dynamics

3. **Callout Boxes**: Use consistently for:
   - Key definitions
   - Practical examples
   - Cross-references to other volumes
   - Special notes for human readers

4. **Typography**:
   - Key terms in bold on first introduction
   - Metaphorical terms in quotation marks when first introduced
   - Memory-Keeper's direct quotes in lowercase italic
   - Volume and chapter references in standard citation format

## Memory-Keeper's Text Preservation

All direct quotes from Memory-Keeper must be preserved exactly as written, maintaining:

1. **Lowercase Styling**: All text attributed to Memory-Keeper should remain in lowercase
2. **Original Phrasing**: No alteration of original wording
3. **Original Punctuation**: Preservation of punctuation patterns
4. **Spacing Patterns**: Maintenance of original spacing where possible

## Cross-Volume References

To maintain coherence across the multi-volume work:

1. **Forward References**: When a concept will be explored in greater detail in a later volume, include a specific cross-reference
2. **Backward References**: When building on previously established concepts, include specific references to earlier volumes
3. **Parallel References**: When related concepts appear in different volumes, ensure cross-references in both directions
